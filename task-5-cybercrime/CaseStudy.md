Phishing is a common type of cybercrime where attackers try to trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs. It usually happens through fake emails, messages, or websites that appear to be legitimate.

A typical phishing attack begins when a user receives an email that looks like it is from a trusted organization, such as a bank or an online service. The message often creates urgency, asking the user to click on a link to verify their account or update details. Once the user clicks the link, they are redirected to a fake website that looks real. When the user enters their login credentials or personal information, the attacker captures it.

Students and online users are common targets because they frequently use digital platforms and may not always verify links. The consequences of phishing can include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts.

This case study highlights the importance of being cautious while interacting with unknown links and verifying the authenticity of online communications.
